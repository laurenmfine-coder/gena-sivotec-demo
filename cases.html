What Are You
Actually Looking At?

Fifteen patients. Three common chief complaints. Within each family, cases share the same opening — and diverge in ways that matter. Your job is to reason from the details, not the diagnosis.

Before you begin: Cases within a family share the same chief complaint. Some share the same diagnosis but present differently. Some look identical and resolve to opposite answers. There are no labels. Reason from clinical evidence — not from what you expect.
