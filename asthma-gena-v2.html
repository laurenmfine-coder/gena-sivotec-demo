The Problem

One protocol for six diseases.
And the gap grows at every step.

Asthma is not one disease. The same chief complaint — wheeze, dyspnea, cough — emerges from at least six distinct inflammatory phenotypes with different biology, different optimal treatments, and different trajectories. Current care applies a one-size-fits-all stepwise protocol at every severity level, from the first SABA prescription to biologic selection. The result: patients accumulate unnecessary OCS exposure, wrong medications, and years of uncontrolled disease.

5–10%
Have severe asthma Uncontrolled despite high-dose ICS-LABA. Candidates for biologic therapy. The population everyone focuses on. But they reached severe because phenotyping failed them earlier.
~40%
Have moderate persistent asthma Failing Step 2–3. The critical decision point — ICS/LABA vs. ICS/LTRA vs. LAMA add-on. Wrong choice here accelerates progression to severe. Phenotype would change the decision.
~55%
Have mild asthma Receiving empiric first-line therapy with no phenotype assessment. FeNO and blood eosinophils at first visit would identify who needs ICS urgently — and who needs something else entirely.

What the Literature Says

The evidence is graded by severity.
So is the opportunity.

An honest review of the evidence finds strong RCT-backed support for phenotype-guided treatment at Step 5, emerging evidence at Steps 3–4, and weak-but-promising signals at Steps 1–2. The research tools exist. They have never been converted to clinical products. That is the gap GENA fills — starting where the evidence is strongest.

🔬
Grade A Severe Asthma — Biologic Selection
Blood eosinophils + FeNO together are the strongest validated predictors of biologic response — better than either alone. ISAR real-world data (35,000 patients, 28 countries) confirms higher pre-biologic BEC and FeNO are associated with greater post-biologic FEV₁ improvement for anti-IgE and anti-IL-5/5R agents. → ISAR registry · CHEST 2025 · Frontiers Immunology 2024 The ISAR eosinophil gradient algorithm (Grade 0–3) was validated in this population using: peak BEC ever, FeNO ever ≥25 ppb, nasal polyps, adult onset, and OCS use. It is the only validated multi-component phenotyping tool in asthma — and it has never been implemented as a clinical product. → CHEST 2021 · npj Primary Care Resp Med 2025
🫁
Grade A FeNO — ICS Response Prediction
FeNO predicts corticosteroid responsiveness more consistently than spirometry, bronchodilator response, peak flow variation, or methacholine AHR (ATS Clinical Practice Guideline, 2011 — endorsed by AAAAI/ACAAI). → AUC 0.89 and 0.86 at 4 and 12 weeks for ICS response prediction (Smith et al.) · FeNO <27 ppb predicts non-response RCT meta-analysis (14 trials): FeNO-guided management algorithms reduced exacerbation risk vs. no FeNO — High strength of evidence (AHRQ Comparative Effectiveness Review 197, 2017). NIOX VERO device is the only commercial FeNO measurement tool — it delivers a number, with no integrated decision logic. → AHRQ CER 197 · ATS FeNO Guideline 2011 · NICE 2024
⚠️
Grade B Rare Disease Exclusion — Expert Consensus
EGPA screening threshold (blood eos >1,500 in asthma), A1AT exclusion criteria (basilar emphysema + normal T2 biomarkers + recurrent infections), and VCD identification (stridor + normal FeNO + spirometry pattern) are supported by expert consensus and specialty guidelines — not RCTs. → 2022 ACR/EULAR EGPA criteria · ATS A1AT statement · GINA 2024 difficult asthma guide No existing clinical tool systematically screens for these masqueraders before biologic selection. This is GENA's unique layer.
📊
Grade B Moderate Asthma — Phenotype-Guided Step-Up
The ISAR eosinophil gradient algorithm was successfully applied to a UK primary care cohort (Clinical Practice Research Datalink): eosinophilic phenotype predominates in primary care and is associated with greater severity and HCRU. "These patients may benefit from earlier and targeted therapy" (PubMed 34403837, 2021). → JACI In Practice 2021 · npj PCRM 2025 T2-low identification at Step 3–4 is critically important: ICS escalation in T2-low patients is ineffective and may worsen neutrophilic inflammation. Tiotropium add-on shows better outcomes in non-eosinophilic phenotype. Evidence base: observational and RCT subgroup analyses — not prospective phenotype-guided RCTs. → GINA 2024 Step 4 guidance · Heaney et al.
🌱
Grade C Mild Asthma — Emerging Evidence
The 2025 npj Primary Care Respiratory Medicine study adapted the ISAR algorithm for a mild asthma population: adding FeNO improved classification (C-statistic 0.978 vs 0.888 for eos alone). However, "FeNO's susceptibility to transient elevations suggests limited added value for routine clinical classification" in mild asthma. → npj PCRM Oct 2025 · GINA 2025 Appendix on T2 biomarkers GINA 2025 added a new appendix on T2 biomarkers across all severity levels — the clearest signal yet that guideline bodies are moving toward phenotype-guided care at every step. The evidence is ahead of the tools. → This is Phase 3 territory. GENA generates the RCT evidence through real-world data collection.
💊
Grade B LTRA Selection — Phenotype Matters
Montelukast: phenotype-specific indications supported by evidence. AERD (nasal polyps + NSAID sensitivity): strong mechanistic rationale — leukotriene pathway is the primary driver. Allergic rhinitis + asthma: treats both simultaneously. Exercise-induced bronchoconstriction: superior to SABA pre-treatment in some trials. Non-allergic eosinophilic asthma: limited evidence — LTRA adds little. GINA 2024 now requires shared decision-making for LTRA due to neuropsychiatric risk — making phenotype guidance even more important. → GINA 2024/2025 · MHRA 2024 safety advice on montelukast

Research Tools Ready for Clinical Translation
ISAR Eosinophil Gradient Algorithm
International Severe Asthma Registry · 35,000 patients · 28 countries
Multicomponent Grade 0–3 phenotyping algorithm using peak BEC, FeNO ever ≥25 ppb, nasal polyps, adult onset, and OCS use. Validated in severe asthma; successfully applied to primary care cohorts in 2021 and 2025. Consensus-built by expert committee. Free to use.
Gap: Never deployed as a clinical decision support tool.
AHRQ FeNO Management Algorithms
Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality · CER 197 · 14 RCTs meta-analysis
Systematic review confirming FeNO-guided management reduces exacerbation risk (High SOE). Step-up, step-down, and adherence monitoring algorithms described. NIOX-specific ICS titration guidance published. Available on AHRQ CDS Connect platform.
Gap: FeNO algorithm exists on paper. No integration with biologic selection or rare disease screening.
SARP / U-BIOPRED Cluster Definitions
Severe Asthma Research Program · Unbiased Biomarkers for Respiratory Disease Outcome
Landmark ML cluster studies establishing T2-high and T2-low endotype consistency across US and European cohorts. Identified three major clinically relevant clusters. Validated biomarker cut-points. Informs biologic trial inclusion criteria.
Gap: Research models only. No clinical tool implements these clusters for real patients.
ACR/EULAR EGPA Classification Criteria
American College of Rheumatology · 2022 · Vasculitis criteria
2022 criteria for EGPA: validated 6-item scoring system including asthma, eosinophilia, nasal polyps, neuropathy, biopsy findings, and ANCA status. Eosinophil threshold of >1,000–1,500/μL in a patient with asthma mandates EGPA evaluation.
Gap: No asthma management tool systematically applies these criteria before escalating therapy.

ISAR · 35,000 patients · 28 countries · 2017–2024
Eosinophil Gradient Algorithm
Expert-consensus multicomponent phenotyping tool: Grade 0–3 eosinophilic likelihood based on peak BEC, FeNO ≥25 ppb, nasal polyps, adult onset, OCS use. Validated in severe asthma cohort (n=1,716). Applied to UK primary care (n=multiple large cohorts). Free to use.
→ Grade 3: eos ≥300 + FeNO ≥25 ppb + polyps + adult onset = highest eosinophilic likelihood. Predicts biologic response.
Gap: Algorithm described in CHEST 2021. Never deployed as a clinical product in any EHR or point-of-care tool.
💨
ATS 2011 · AHRQ CER 197 · 14 RCTs · High SOE
FeNO Management Algorithms
FeNO-guided management reduces exacerbation risk vs. usual care (14 RCTs, High SOE). FeNO >47 ppb predicts ICS response (AUC 0.89 at 4 weeks). FeNO <27 ppb predicts non-response. Change of >10 ppb or >20% = clinically significant. ICS suppresses FeNO in days — useful for adherence monitoring.
→ FeNO persistently high despite ICS = non-adherence OR biologic-eligible steroid-resistant T2 inflammation.
Gap: NIOX VERO delivers a number. No device integrates FeNO into a step-decision algorithm at the point of care.
🔬
SARP · U-BIOPRED · ADEPT · Large RCT meta-analyses
Endotype Cluster Definitions
Consistent T2-high / T2-low cluster definitions across three independent research programs. T2-high: early allergic, late eosinophilic. T2-low: neutrophilic, obesity-driven, mixed. Biomarker cut-points validated. These define the treatment response boundaries used in biologic trial inclusion criteria.
→ T2-low patients show worse ICS response, better tiotropium response, and are biologic non-responders to IL-5/IL-13 agents.
Gap: Research cluster definitions. No clinical tool implements them for individual patients at the point of care.
⚠️
ACR/EULAR 2022 · GINA Difficult Asthma Guide · ATS A1AT Statement
Rare Disease Threshold Rules
Validated screening criteria for EGPA (eos >1,000–1,500 + organ involvement in asthma patient), A1AT deficiency (basilar emphysema + normal T2 + recurrent infections + family COPD), and VCD/ILO (stridor + normal FeNO + spirometry pattern). Published in specialty guidelines.
→ Applying these criteria before biologic initiation changes the clinical decision in 3–5% of "severe asthma" patients — but those cases have the highest stakes.
Gap: No asthma management tool systematically applies rare disease criteria before escalating therapy.
💊
CHEST 2025 · JACI In Practice 2023 · LIBERTY ASTHMA trials
Biologic Selection Evidence Matrix
Individual biologic trial data + real-world outcomes (ISAR) + expert consensus frameworks (CHEST 2025 "How I Do It" series) provide a structured basis for ranked biologic selection. No head-to-head trials exist. Evidence is biomarker-stratified subgroup analysis + real-world registry data.
→ Higher pre-biologic BEC + FeNO → greater post-biologic FEV₁ improvement for anti-IgE and anti-IL-5/5R. ISAR real-world confirmation.
Gap: Evidence exists in papers and expert opinion. No tool synthesizes it into a ranked recommendation with explicit rationale.

NHLBI published CDS Connect rules for Steps 2–5 transitions in HL7 FHIR-compatible format. This infrastructure is built. The Step 2–5 rules are deployed. The biologic selection layer — where GENA lives — was explicitly out of scope for the original NHLBI project.
